About Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects movement. It occurs when nerve cells (neurons) in the brain gradually break down or die, leading to a shortage of dopamine, a chemical messenger involved in controlling movement.

Symptoms

Tremors: Involuntary shaking, usually starting in one hand.

Bradykinesia: Slowed movement and difficulty initiating and executing movements.

Muscle rigidity: Stiffness or inflexibility in the muscles, often leading to decreased range of motion.

Postural instability: Impaired balance and coordination, increasing the risk of falls.

Impaired speech and writing: Speaking or writing may become softer, slurred, or illegible.

Causes

Loss of dopamine-producing neurons: The exact cause of Parkinson's disease is unknown, but genetics, environmental factors, and aging are believed to play a role.

Alpha-synuclein accumulation: Clumps of protein called Lewy bodies accumulate in the brain, disrupting normal cell function.

Oxidative stress: Damage to cells caused by free radicals may contribute to neuron degeneration.

Inflammation: Chronic inflammation in the brain may contribute to Parkinson's disease progression.

Treatment Options

Medications: Dopamine agonists, levodopa, and other medications can help manage motor symptoms and improve quality of life.

Deep brain stimulation (DBS): Surgical implantation of electrodes in the brain can help regulate abnormal brain activity and reduce symptoms.

Physical therapy: Exercises and stretching can improve flexibility, balance, and mobility.

Speech therapy: Techniques to improve speech and swallowing difficulties.

Occupational therapy: Strategies to enhance daily living activities and maintain independence.

Complications

Motor fluctuations: Fluctuations in medication effectiveness can lead to "on-off" periods, where symptoms vary in severity throughout the day.

Dyskinesia: Involuntary, jerky movements or writhing motions, often as a side effect of long-term levodopa use.

Cognitive changes: Parkinson's disease can lead to memory problems, confusion, and difficulty concentrating, especially in later stages.

Psychiatric symptoms: Depression, anxiety, hallucinations, and psychosis may occur, impacting mental well-being and quality of life.

Swallowing and digestive issues: Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia), constipation, and other gastrointestinal problems can arise, increasing the risk of malnutrition and aspiration pneumonia.
