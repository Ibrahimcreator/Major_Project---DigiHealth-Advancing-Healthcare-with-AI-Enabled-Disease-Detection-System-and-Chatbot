About Heart Disease

Heart disease, also known as cardiovascular disease (CVD), encompasses various conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. It's a leading cause of death worldwide.

Symptoms

  • Palpitations (racing or pounding heart): Sensations of a fluttering or irregular heartbeat.
  • Chest pain or discomfort: Tightness, pressure, or discomfort in the chest area.
  • Sweating: Perspiration, often accompanied by other symptoms.
  • Lightheadedness or dizziness: Feeling faint or unsteady.
  • Shortness of breath: Difficulty breathing, particularly during exertion.

Causes

  • Atherosclerosis (plaque buildup in arteries): Narrowing of arteries due to cholesterol and fatty deposits.
  • Hypertension (high blood pressure): Increased force of blood against artery walls.
  • Diabetes: Elevated blood sugar levels damaging blood vessels.
  • Obesity: Excess body weight straining the heart and arteries.
  • Smoking: Chemicals in tobacco damaging blood vessels and increasing clotting.
  • Sedentary lifestyle: Lack of physical activity leading to poor cardiovascular health.

Treatment Options

  • Lifestyle modifications: Adopting a heart-healthy diet, regular exercise, smoking cessation, stress management, and weight control.
  • Medications: Prescribed to manage risk factors such as high blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels.
  • Medical procedures: Surgical interventions like angioplasty, stent placement, bypass surgery, or valve repair to improve blood flow and heart function.

Complications

  • Heart attack (myocardial infarction): Damage to the heart muscle due to blocked blood flow.
  • Stroke: Interruption of blood supply to the brain, leading to brain damage.
  • Heart failure: Inability of the heart to pump enough blood to meet the body's needs.
  • Peripheral artery disease: Narrowing of arteries in the limbs, reducing blood flow and causing pain or numbness.
  • Sudden cardiac arrest: Abrupt loss of heart function, often due to an electrical disturbance.
